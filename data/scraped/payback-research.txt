OVERVIEW
The Payback series represents Thinkbox's landmark longitudinal research into TV advertising's return on investment, analysing thousands of campaigns across multiple years. Payback 4 (2014) is the most comprehensive edition, covering 4,500+ campaigns and providing the most granular sector and seasonal analysis of TV ROI available.

KEY DATASET
- 4,500+ campaigns analysed across Payback series
- Multiple sectors and advertiser sizes
- Short-term and long-term payback measured
- Seasonal patterns documented by sector

CORE ROI FINDINGS

Overall TV ROI
TV advertising consistently generates positive returns across all measured sectors. Short-term ROI (within campaign period) averages around £1.70-£1.90 per £1 spent, with long-term effects (up to 3 years) driving total returns significantly higher.

Long-Term vs Short-Term Effects
A defining finding across the Payback series: the majority of TV's value accrues after the campaign ends. Short-term direct response accounts for a minority of total profit contribution. Long-term brand-building effects — which accumulate over months and years — represent the larger portion. This means measuring TV only on immediate response dramatically underestimates its true value.

Sector ROI Variation
ROI varies significantly by sector, reflecting product margins, purchase frequency, and category sensitivity to advertising:
- FMCG (fast-moving consumer goods): Strong short-term ROI from high purchase frequency; advertising drives both immediate sales and long-term loyalty
- Retail: Clear attribution from promotional campaigns; strong immediate payback, particularly during seasonal peaks
- Financial Services: Lower short-term ROI due to longer consideration cycles, but high lifetime customer value means long-term returns are substantial
- Automotive: Longer consideration periods; advertising effectiveness measured over extended windows; high-value transactions amplify ROI
- Telecoms: Competitive category; TV effective for brand differentiation and switching

SEASONAL WINDOWS BY SECTOR
TV advertising performs differently across the calendar year. Optimal investment periods:
- Retail: Pre-Christmas (October-December) delivers strongest ROI; summer also strong for fashion/lifestyle
- FMCG: Summer months (July-August) optimal for many categories; pre-Christmas for food and gifting
- Financial Services: January (new year financial decisions), February-March (ISA season)
- Automotive: March and September (registration plate changes drive purchase intent); spring generally strong
- Travel: January-February (holiday booking season)

Planning around these seasonal windows significantly improves ROI and media efficiency.

BUDGET SCALING
Payback research demonstrates TV's ROI holds across budget sizes, from relatively modest campaigns to large multi-million pound investments. However, achieving scale above minimum effective thresholds is critical — campaigns below reach thresholds generate disproportionately lower returns. The research supports a "go heavy or don't go" approach for many sectors, particularly those with short campaign windows.

MEDIA MIX EVIDENCE
TV works best as part of an integrated media plan. The Payback series documents uplift in other channels' performance when TV is part of the mix:
- Paid search campaigns perform better when TV is running concurrently
- Online display response rates improve with TV support
- TV creates the "rising tide" effect, lifting all channels

LONG-TERM BRAND BUILDING
Beyond direct sales ROI, the Payback research documents TV's contribution to brand equity metrics that sustain long-term competitive advantage:
- Brand awareness and spontaneous recall
- Brand consideration and preference
- Price elasticity reduction (consumers less price-sensitive to brands they recognise from TV)
- New customer acquisition rates
These effects compound over years, making the true multi-year ROI of consistent TV investment substantially higher than any single-year measurement captures.
