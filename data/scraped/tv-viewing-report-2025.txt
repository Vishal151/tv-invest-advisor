OVERVIEW
Annual report on UK television viewing patterns, covering Total TV (broadcast TV plus broadcaster video on demand). Data sourced from BARB (Broadcasters' Audience Research Board).

KEY STATISTICS 2025

Daily Viewing
In 2025, UK adults watched 3 hours 26 minutes of Total TV each day on average.

Video on Demand Among Young Adults
VOD represents 69% of Total TV viewing for 16-34 year olds, indicating a substantial shift toward on-demand content among younger demographics.

TV Advertising Dominance
Total TV advertising accounted for 85% of the video advertising seen each day in 2025, demonstrating television's continued dominance in the video advertising landscape.

Young Adult TV Advertising Engagement
The 16-34 age group spend more time with TV advertising than with YouTube and TikTok's combined output, challenging assumptions about young adult media consumption.

CONTEXT
These figures represent Total TV, which includes both traditional broadcast viewing and broadcaster VOD (BVOD) platforms such as ITV X, Channel 4, and Sky. The data demonstrates that despite fragmentation in viewing habits, TV as a platform — including its digital extensions — maintains dominant share of both viewing time and advertising exposure.

For media planners: TV's reach across all demographics remains unmatched. Even among 16-34s, where VOD dominates the format mix, the platform retains more advertising attention than all major social video platforms combined.
